How much for health and social care?

The British government has claimed that it has given the NHS £10 billion more to help fund the service to 2020/21. Our analysis has showed that figure is much closer to £4.5 billion, or even just £800 million if we look at health-specific inflation.

Meanwhile, during the campaign to leave the EU, more cash was touted for the NHS on the scale of £350 million per week. That's figure that's been highly contested and disavowed by Leave campaigners after the referendum result.

What is not contested is that by 2020/21, the health care sector in the the UK is expected to face a £30 billion gap between its funding and what it will cost to run based on increasing demand from a growing and ageing population.

To close that gap, the NHS has been asked to find £22 billion in efficiency savings.
